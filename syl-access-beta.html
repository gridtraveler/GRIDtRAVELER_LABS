Setup & First Launch
01
Extract & Place at C:\SYL\
Extract the ZIP, then move the SYL folder to your C:\ drive root.

The final path must be exactly: C:\SYL\

Confirm it looks like this before continuing:
C:\SYL\01_INSTALL_FIRST.bat
C:\SYL\02_START_SYL.bat

Do not place it on the Desktop, Documents, or any subfolder. The system will not launch from any other location.
02
Install Dependencies (Once)
Open the SYL folder. Right-click 01_INSTALL_FIRST.bat → Run as Administrator. This installs Python, required packages, and configures the control server. A terminal window opens and closes automatically when complete. This step only needs to be done once.
03
Your Media Folder
Inside the SYL folder you'll find two show folders:

SHOW_FOLDER_MASTERMEDIAFOLDER — pre-configured and ready to use immediately. Drop your MP4 or MOV files directly into it and you're set.

SHOW_FOLDER_TEMPLATE — an empty template for future shows. When you're comfortable with the workflow, copy this template to any location on your machine, fill it with your media files, then point SYL to it using the folder selector at startup. Your show folder can live on any drive.
04
Launch SYL
Double-click 02_START_SYL.bat. The control surface opens automatically in your browser and the SYL engine starts. Allow 60–90 seconds for the engine to fully initialize on first run. Do not close the terminal window that appears — it keeps the system running. Your show folder is scanned automatically and assets appear in the Media Browser.
05
Operating SYL
Media Browser — Your video assets load as thumbnails. Click any clip to select it.

Assign to Surfaces — SYL drives three surfaces: L · M · R (Left, Main, Right). With a clip selected, click the surface button to assign. Each surface is independent.

Cues 1–4 — Select a cue slot and assign clips to any combination of surfaces. Press ARM to prepare the cue silently — nothing changes on output until you fire. Press FIRE to execute. All assigned surfaces cut simultaneously. Cue assignments are saved automatically between sessions.

Presets P1–P4 — Store and recall full surface states instantly. Press a preset slot to write your current surface configuration to it. Press it again any time to restore that state. Use for fast scene switching mid-show.

Audio / Dropboard — Drag a WAV file directly onto the Dropboard panel, or use the file picker. Play, pause, and level control are available in the panel. Audio runs independently of the video surfaces.

REC — Press to start recording the current SYL output. Press again to stop. Recordings are saved as timestamped MP4 files in the logs folder inside your SYL directory. Open that folder to retrieve them.
Interface Reference
MEDIA BROWSER Displays all video assets from your active show folder as thumbnails. Click to select a clip before assigning or previewing.
SURFACE L / M / R Left, Main, and Right LED wall outputs. Each surface is independently assigned and can run different content simultaneously.
CUE 1 – 4 Four programmable cue slots. Each stores a complete surface assignment. ARM prepares silently. FIRE executes the cut live.
PRESET P1 – P4 Full-state snapshots. Saves and recalls your entire surface layout instantly. Designed for fast live scene switching.
DROPBOARD / AUDIO WAV audio playback. Drag files directly onto the panel. Independent volume, play, and pause controls.
REC Records current SYL output to MP4. Files are saved to the logs folder with a timestamp filename. Press once to start, again to stop.
SAVE Writes current cue and surface state to your show file. Sessions also auto-save on clean exit.
